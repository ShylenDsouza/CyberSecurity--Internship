3. What I Learnt
   
Phishing is a type of cyber attack where attackers attempt to trick individuals into providing sensitive information such as passwords, bank details, or OTPs by pretending to be a trusted source.

Types of Phishing:

* Email Phishing: Fake emails that appear to be from legitimate organizations
* Spear Phishing: Targeted phishing attacks aimed at specific individuals
* Smishing: Phishing attacks conducted through SMS messages

How Phishing Works:

Attackers create fake emails, messages, or websites that look genuine. These often contain malicious links or attachments. When users click on these links or provide their information, attackers gain access to sensitive data.

How to Identify Phishing:

* Suspicious or unknown sender email addresses
* Urgent or threatening messages
* Links that do not match official websites
* Requests for sensitive information

Prevention Methods:

* Always verify the sender before responding
* Avoid clicking on unknown links or attachments
* Enable Two-Factor Authentication (2FA)
* Use updated antivirus and security tools

5. Real-World Example

A common example of phishing is receiving an email that appears to be from a bank asking to "verify your account details." The email contains a link to a fake website that looks identical to the real bank’s website. When users enter their login details, attackers capture the information and misuse it.
