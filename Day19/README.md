Perspective Shift

1. One belief I had before starting:
Before starting this program, I believed that cybersecurity was mainly about hacking tools and attacking systems. I thought learning a few popular tools would be enough to become good at cybersecurity quickly.

2. Why that belief is not fully correct:
Now I understand that cybersecurity is much broader than just hacking. It includes networking, system security, monitoring, analysis, risk awareness, and problem-solving. Tools are helpful, but they only work well when the person using them understands the basics. Without strong fundamentals, it is difficult to know what the tool is doing or how to interpret the results properly.

3. One reason instant results are not realistic:
Instant results are not realistic because cybersecurity requires time, repetition, and practical experience. It is not a field where I can master everything just by watching videos or reading theory once. I need regular practice, hands-on learning, and patience to improve step by step.

4. One realistic expectation I now have:
One realistic expectation I now have is that I do not need to learn everything at once. My goal is to improve consistently, strengthen my fundamentals, and become better over time through daily effort, practice, and continuous learning.

Final Reflection:
This program helped me change my mindset. Earlier, I looked at cybersecurity as something fast and tool-based, but now I see it as a long-term journey that requires discipline, curiosity, and consistency. I now understand that real growth comes from building strong basics and learning through practice.
