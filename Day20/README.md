Learning Summary & Next Plan

1. My Top 5 Learnings from This Program:

->Cybersecurity is built on strong fundamentals:
I learned that understanding networking, ports, protocols, operating systems, and basic security concepts is very important. Without fundamentals, advanced topics become confusing.

->Practical learning is more effective than only theory:
This program showed me that reading and watching videos are useful, but hands-on practice helps me understand concepts much better. Practical work improves confidence and problem-solving.

->Consistency matters more than speed:
I understood that learning cybersecurity is not about finishing quickly. Small daily progress is more valuable than trying to learn everything in one day and getting overwhelmed.

->Tools do not replace knowledge
Earlier, I thought security tools would do most of the work. Now I know tools only support the analyst. Real understanding comes from knowing what to look for, why something happens, and how to respond.

->Cybersecurity has many paths
Through this journey, I understood that cybersecurity is a wide field with multiple areas like networking, SOC, DFIR, web security, GRC, and more. This helped me think seriously about where I want to focus.

2. Three Mistakes I Will Not Repeat

->Depending too much on passive learning:
I will not depend only on videos and theory. I will make sure to combine learning with labs, practice, and note-making.

->Trying to learn too many things at once
I will avoid jumping between too many topics without a clear plan. Instead, I will focus on one area properly and build depth step by step.

->Ignoring revision and consistency
I will not study randomly and forget earlier topics. I will revise regularly and stay consistent so that my knowledge becomes stronger over time.
